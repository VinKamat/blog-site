### [@BrianRoemmele](https://x.com/BrianRoemmele/status/2028524908779802736)

A lone developer cracked open Apple's Neural Engine for full neural network training — backpropagation included — without CoreML, Metal, or GPU. Single transformer layer (dim=768, seq=512) in 9.3ms at 1.78 TFLOPS with only 11.2% ANE utilization on an M4. The ANE hits ~6.6 TFLOPS per watt — 80x more efficient than an A100.

> The framing of "your Mac is now an AI supercomputer" is hype, but the underlying number isn't: 80x efficiency per watt is a real architectural advantage. If this scales to full models, the calculus for on-device fine-tuning flips completely. Worth following closely — this is the kind of breakthrough that rewrites infrastructure assumptions over 12–18 months.

### [@ompsychiatrist](https://x.com/ompsychiatrist/status/2028033437089820768)

A 2024 EEG study found higher short-video addiction correlates with reduced theta activity in the prefrontal cortex — the region responsible for attention and self-control. JAMA Pediatrics (2023) linked excessive early screen exposure to delayed language and executive development. Short-form feeds condition the brain to crave novelty, shortening attention and raising irritability over time.

> This is the scientific backing for what most people sense but dismiss. Lower prefrontal theta = measurably weaker attention and impulse control. Worth holding next to the dopamine thread from March 3: these two pieces of research together form a pretty clear picture of what algorithmic feeds are doing at the neurological level. The attention economy has an actual cost, measurable in EEGs.
