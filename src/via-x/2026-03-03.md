### [@aakashgupta](https://x.com/aakashgupta/status/2028726209237098553)

Every time you swipe to a new 30-second video, your brain releases a dopamine pulse. 270 times per day. Average attention span dropped from 12 seconds (2015) to 8.25 seconds (2025). After TikTok interruption in a task study, accuracy dropped to near-random. Twitter and YouTube showed zero impact. The short-form feed format specifically degrades prospective memory — your ability to hold an intention across a time gap.

> The resistance training framing is the one that sticks. The discomfort you feel 10 minutes into a book after heavy scrolling isn't weakness — it's the same feeling as rep 8 of a hard set. The adaptation is on the other side of it. Most people stop before finding out.
