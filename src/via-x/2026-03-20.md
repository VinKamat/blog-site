### [@aakashgupta](https://x.com/aakashgupta/status/2034530813006520669)

On the death of the PRD: "The spec moved from step 2 to step 6." Old flow: Idea → PRD → Design → Eng → QA → Ship (8-12 weeks). New flow: Idea → 5 prototypes → Evaluate → Kill 4 → Spec the survivor → Ship (1-2 weeks). The PRD is now a decision record, not a permission document. Anthropic has no PRDs. OpenAI still uses specs as behavior contracts with labeled examples. Enterprises use documents as alignment mechanisms across time zones.

> This maps directly to how AI-assisted development actually works in practice. When you can prototype 5 things in the time it used to take to write one PRD, the whole point of the document changes. The insight about company stage determining where the spec sits is worth keeping — it's why the "no PRDs" advice from startups doesn't translate directly to large enterprise teams.

### [@AndrewWarner](https://x.com/AndrewWarner/status/2034655164431241655)

Marik Hazan's Feltsense raised $5.1M for an AI that has "built" 1,000+ startups: scrapes social for pain points → assigns to AI "founders" → builds landing pages → buys ads to test demand → calls waitlist members → kills, pivots, or doubles down based on feedback.

> The architecture here is interesting regardless of whether the product works: it's a full agentic loop with real-world feedback signals (ad spend, phone calls, conversion rates) closing the iteration cycle. Most agentic demos have humans in the loop at every evaluation step. This one is trying to make the feedback loop fully autonomous. Worth watching whether the unit economics actually work once you're past the "1,000 landing pages" stage.
