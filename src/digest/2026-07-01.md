### [What's new in Claude Sonnet 5](https://platform.claude.com/docs/en/about-claude/models/whats-new-sonnet-5)
Anthropic released Claude Sonnet 5 as a drop-in upgrade for Sonnet 4.6 with a 1M token context window, 128k max output, and adaptive thinking enabled by default. The launch also removes manual thinking budgets and non-default sampling parameters, while a new tokenizer drives token counts roughly 30% higher for the same text.
> This is the kind of release that looks simple until you hit prod. Teams with tight token budgets or temperature-tuned workloads need migration tests before they swap model IDs.

### [Trump drops restrictions on Anthropic’s Mythos and Fable models](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/trump-drops-restrictions-on-anthropics-mythos-and-fable-models/)
The U.S. lifted the license requirement that had blocked Anthropic from broadly exporting Mythos and Fable, and Anthropic said access would start returning on Wednesday, July 1. The models had been cut off after a June 12 export control move made broad public availability impractical.
> Policy is now part of model rollout risk. If you are betting on frontier models for enterprise workflows, availability and governance can change faster than your backlog.

### [Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite Image – Nano Banana 2 Lite](https://deepmind.google/models/gemini-image/flash-lite/)
Google DeepMind introduced Nano Banana 2 Lite as a faster, cheaper image model aimed at high-volume generation and editing. Google is positioning it around low latency, lower per-image cost, and enough control to support product workflows that need rapid iteration.
> Cheap, fast image generation keeps moving from demo candy into workflow infrastructure. The interesting part is where it fits into design tools, learning products, and agent-generated collateral.

### [X now offers an MCP server to make its platform easier for AI tools to use](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/x-now-offers-an-mcp-server-to-make-its-platform-easier-for-ai-tools-to-use/)
X launched a hosted MCP server that lets compatible AI tools use a person’s X account permissions for search, reading, and analysis without developers having to build their own bridge. X told TechCrunch the server does not expose write endpoints, so it cannot post autonomously.
> Official MCP endpoints keep removing glue code from enterprise pilots. That shifts the bottleneck from integration to governance, rate limits, and whether the data is worth wiring into decisions.
</div>

### [Amazon launches new $1 billion FDE org, following OpenAI and Anthropic](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/30/amazon-launches-new-1-billion-fde-org-following-openai-and-anthropic/)
AWS launched a new internal forward-deployed engineering organization and says it is committing $1 billion in internal resources to it. The team will embed with customers to deploy purpose-built agents and leave behind reusable skills, workflows, and engineering patterns.
> This is a strong signal that agent delivery is turning into a services business. The platform matters, and the hard part is still workflow design, change management, and getting teams to own the system after launch.
</div>

### [AIEWF Daily Dispatch: Loops, Software Factories & Forward Deployed Engineers](https://www.latent.space/p/aiewf-daily-dispatch-loops)
Latent Space’s day-two dispatch from AI Engineer World’s Fair says the big themes were loops, software factories, forward-deployed engineers, and more work happening in the orchestration layer than in the models themselves. Speakers from Microsoft, OpenAI, Warp, Sierra, and others framed persistent agent loops as the next step after chat and single-tool workflows.
> The center of gravity is shifting from single prompts to operating loops. Architecture work is moving up a level toward orchestration, review paths, and signal collection that keep agents useful over time.
