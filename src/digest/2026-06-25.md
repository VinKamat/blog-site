### [Prompt Injection as Role Confusion](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/22/prompt-injection-as-role-confusion/)
Simon Willison highlighted new research arguing that models struggle to separate trusted role text from untrusted user text. The paper's "destyling" result is the sharpest detail: small wording changes cut attack success from 61% to 10%.
> This hits the core weakness in agent systems that read email, web pages, tickets, and docs. If style can move model behavior that much, enterprises need stronger isolation, narrower permissions, and less faith in prompt-only defenses.
</div>

### [[AINews] It's Meta-Harness Summer](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-its-meta-harness-summer)
Latent.Space says a new control layer is forming around agents, with projects like Omnigent, Eve, HarnessAgent, Flue, and OpenInspect all pushing toward a shared harness pattern. The piece frames this as the next architectural battleground for secure, pluggable, scalable agent systems.
> The harness is turning into the real product surface. Model quality still matters, but the durable advantage is moving toward identity, policy, observability, memory, and cost control across many agents.
</div>

### [OpenAI unveils its first custom chip, built by Broadcom](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/openai-unveils-its-first-custom-chip-built-by-broadcom/)
OpenAI introduced Jalapeño, its first custom inference chip, built with Broadcom and aimed at ChatGPT, Codex, API traffic, and future agent products. The company says early testing shows significantly better performance per watt than current alternatives.
> This is full-stack AI in plain view. When a model vendor starts shaping silicon around inference workloads, pricing, latency, and capacity planning become product features instead of vendor constraints.
</div>

### [Anthropic’s Claude Tag is learning your company, one Slack message at a time](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/23/anthropics-claude-tag-is-learning-your-company-one-slack-message-at-a-time/)
Anthropic's Claude Tag brings an always-on Claude identity into Slack, with shared channel memory, scoped permissions, and the ability to take on staged tasks in public threads. It also has an ambient mode that can proactively follow up and surface relevant context.
> This pushes enterprise AI toward embedded teammates instead of request-response assistants. The hard part now is governance: memory boundaries, audit trails, access scope, and who owns the organizational graph behind the agent.
</div>

### [Amazon ups India bet with fresh $13B AI infrastructure investment](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/amazon-ups-india-bet-with-fresh-13b-ai-infrastructure-investment/)
Amazon said it will invest another $13 billion in India through 2030 to expand AWS data center capacity in Mumbai and Hyderabad. That brings Amazon's total announced India commitments to $48 billion as global tech firms race to build AI infrastructure in the country.
> Regional AI capacity is becoming a first-order architecture choice. Data residency, latency, and local incentives will shape platform design far more than teams expected a year ago.

### [Companies are scrambling to stop employees from maxing out AI budgets with small tasks](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/24/companies-are-scrambling-to-stop-employees-from-maxing-out-ai-budgets-with-small-tasks/)
TechCrunch reports that companies are starting to clamp down on runaway token spend after pushing employees to use AI heavily earlier this year. Accenture is cited as an example, with leadership questioning whether the spend is delivering enough value.
