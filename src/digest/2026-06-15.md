### [[AINews] Fable and Mythos officially too dangerous to release](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Anthropic suspended access to Fable 5 and Mythos 5 after a U.S. government directive tied to an alleged jailbreak risk. The move spilled into downstream tools and reopened questions about export controls for frontier model APIs.
> This is the cleanest recent example of model sovereignty becoming an architecture issue. If a core model can disappear over a weekend, enterprise teams need fallback providers, looser coupling, and a plan for degraded operation.

### [Artificial Analysis replaced SWE-Bench Pro with DeepSWE in its Coding Agent Index](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Artificial Analysis said it swapped SWE-Bench Pro for DeepSWE to reduce benchmark gaming, and the change reshuffled the top coding-agent rankings. The discussion around the update focused on how much of an agent score comes from the harness versus the underlying model.
> This matters because teams keep treating leaderboard movement like pure model truth. System design, tool wiring, fallback behavior, and eval hygiene are shaping results just as much as raw weights.

### [Moonshot released Kimi-K2.7-Code as an open coding model](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Moonshot announced Kimi-K2.7-Code as an open-source coding model and reported gains over K2.6 on several coding benchmarks with lower reasoning-token use. Early reactions pointed to better efficiency and stronger tooling support on day one.
> Open coding models keep getting more usable for serious engineering teams. The real question now is operational fit: eval quality, inference cost, and whether the model behaves well inside your own agent harness.

### [MiniMax M3 launched as an open-weight multimodal model](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
MiniMax released M3 with an open-weight multimodal design, roughly 428B total parameters, about 23B active parameters, and a 1M-token context window. Inference stacks including vLLM, SGLang, Together, Baseten, and Fireworks moved quickly to support it.
> The fast support story is the signal here. Model launches are starting to matter less on announcement day and more on how quickly the ecosystem can turn a release into something teams can actually run.
</div>

### [Artificial Analysis launched AA-AgentPerf for agentic inference](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Artificial Analysis introduced AA-AgentPerf, a benchmark aimed at long-horizon agent workloads and optimized inference paths such as KV-cache reuse, speculative decoding, and prefill/decode disaggregation. Its framing pushes evaluation closer to throughput, power, and full-system efficiency.
> This is where AI infra conversations are headed. Once agents run longer and call more tools, the winning stack is the one that keeps latency, power, and cost under control across the whole trajectory.
</div>

### [KPMG pulls report on AI usage due to apparent hallucinations](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/13/kpmg-pulls-report-on-ai-usage-due-to-apparent-hallucinations/)
KPMG withdrew an AI usage report after apparent hallucinations showed up in the published material. The incident landed as another public example of weak verification around AI-generated analysis.

### [Why AI hasn’t replaced software engineers, and won’t](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/14/why-ai-hasnt-replaced-software-engineers/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison highlighted an essay by Arvind Narayanan and Sayash Kapoor arguing that software engineering bottlenecks still center on problem framing, verification, and deep context rather than typing code faster. The piece also points to weak evidence for AI-driven mass layoffs so far.
> This lines up with what strong teams are seeing on the ground. AI helps most when it is embedded in engineers who understand the system, the business constraints, and what good output actually looks like.
