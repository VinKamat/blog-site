### [Datasette Apps: Host custom HTML applications inside Datasette](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/18/datasette-apps/)
Simon Willison launched `datasette-apps`, a plugin that runs self-contained HTML and JavaScript apps inside a sandboxed iframe on top of Datasette. The apps can issue read-only SQL queries against Datasette data, and write operations are possible through allow-listed stored queries.
> This is a strong pattern for enterprise AI tooling. It keeps generated UI code close to governed data and puts real browser boundaries around model output.

### [GLM-5.2 is probably the most powerful text-only open weights LLM](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/17/glm-52/)
Z.ai released GLM-5.2 open weights on June 16 under an MIT license after an earlier subscriber launch on June 13. Simon Willison highlights its 753B-parameter MoE design with 40 active parameters, a 1 million token context window, top open-weights ranking at Artificial Analysis, and a strong showing on the Code Arena WebDev leaderboard.
> Open models only change architecture decisions when quality is real. GLM-5.2 looks like one of the few releases that can move self-hosted and multi-vendor plans from theory into active evaluation.

### [Amazon hopes to challenge Nvidia more directly by selling its AI chips](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/amazon-hopes-to-challenge-nvidia-more-directly-by-selling-its-ai-chips/)
AWS says it is in talks to sell Trainium AI chips to other companies for their own data centers. TechCrunch ties that to Andy Jassy’s April claim that Amazon’s chip business could be running at roughly $50 billion annually if sold as a standalone business, even though current and next-gen Trainium capacity is already heavily committed.
> Inference economics are turning into infrastructure strategy. If AWS opens Trainium beyond its own cloud, Nvidia pressure gets more real and enterprise buyers get another path to negotiate around scarcity.
</div>

### [Source: Elastic agrees to buy CRV-backed DeductiveAI for up to $85M](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/source-elastic-agrees-to-buy-crv-backed-deductiveai-for-up-to-85m/)
According to TechCrunch, Elastic has agreed to buy DeductiveAI for up to $85 million. DeductiveAI launched in 2023 to use AI for bug detection and incident resolution, and the deal would fold that AI SRE capability into Elastic’s observability platform.
> Incumbents are buying agentic operations features instead of waiting to build them all in-house. Expect observability platforms to keep absorbing automated triage and remediation.

### [AI data centers just got a government-mandated fast lane to the grid](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/ai-data-centers-just-got-a-government-mandated-fast-lane-to-the-grid/)
FERC ordered six major grid operators to speed up interconnection work for data centers and other large loads, with 30-day capacity reporting and 60-day rate reviews. The order helps queue movement, though it does not solve the broader shortage of generating capacity.
> AI infrastructure is a power-systems problem now. Capacity planning, siting, and energy contracts are moving closer to model strategy than most software teams are used to.
