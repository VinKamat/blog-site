### [How agents are transforming work](https://openai.com/index/how-agents-are-transforming-work/)
OpenAI says Codex has become the primary AI work tool across every department inside the company. By June 2026, median usage among active internal users was up 56x in Research, 32x in Customer Support, 27x in Engineering, and 13x in Legal versus November 2025.
> This is one of the clearest signs yet that agentic workflows are moving out of the demo phase. Teams need architecture for long-running delegated work now: approvals, observability, spend controls, and tighter permission boundaries.

### [The White House is asking OpenAI to slow roll the release of its new model over safety concerns](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/the-white-house-is-asking-openai-to-slow-roll-the-release-of-its-new-model-over-safety-concerns/)
TechCrunch reports that the White House asked OpenAI to limit the initial release of its next model to a select group of partners while safety concerns are reviewed. The article says the broader public rollout is being held back for now.
> Frontier model launches are turning into policy events. If your roadmap depends on instant access to the newest model, you need a fallback plan and a slower release assumption.

### [Patronus AI lands $50M to build ‘digital worlds’ that stress-test AI agents](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/patronus-ai-lands-50m-to-build-digital-worlds-that-stress-test-ai-agents/)
Patronus AI raised $50 million to build simulated environments for evaluating AI agents under more realistic conditions. The company, founded by former Meta AI researchers, is betting that enterprises need stronger pre-production testing than today’s benchmark suites provide.
> This is where serious agent engineering is headed. Static evals miss too much, and sandbox worlds are a better way to surface brittle plans, unsafe tool use, and failure loops before customers do.
</div>

### [AI and Liability](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/25/ai-and-liability/)
Simon Willison highlighted Bruce Schneier’s response to a German ruling that holds Google liable for false statements generated in AI Overviews. The legal argument is straightforward: if a company deploys the system, the company owns the output.
> Risk teams should pay attention here. Once an agent can speak or act on behalf of the business, traceability, review paths, and ownership of bad output become hard architecture requirements.
</div>

### [General Intuition’s $2.3B bet that video games can train AI agents for the real world](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/general-intuitions-2-3b-bet-that-video-games-can-train-ai-agents-for-the-real-world/)
General Intuition has raised $320 million and is valued at $2.3 billion as it trains AI on millions of hours of gameplay. The company’s thesis is that action-rich game data can help agents build stronger real-world intuition and planning ability.
> The data question is widening fast. Text got us this far, and action traces may be the next big advantage for agents that need planning, sequencing, and adaptation under uncertainty.
</div>

### [Amazon ups India bet with fresh $13B AI infrastructure investment](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/amazon-ups-india-bet-with-fresh-13b-ai-infrastructure-investment/)
Amazon said it will invest another $13 billion in India through 2030 to expand AWS data center capacity in Mumbai and Hyderabad. That brings Amazon’s total announced India commitments to $48 billion as AI infrastructure spending keeps accelerating.
> Regional capacity is becoming a first-order design choice. Data residency, latency, sovereign controls, and local incentives are shaping AI platform architecture far earlier in the stack than most teams expected.
</div>
