### [OpenAI limits GPT-5.6 rollout after government request, says restrictions shouldn’t be the norm](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/26/openai-limits-gpt-5-6-rollout-after-government-request-says-restrictions-shouldnt-be-the-norm/)
OpenAI introduced GPT-5.6 as a three-model family: Sol, Terra, and Luna. The company is starting with a limited preview for trusted partners after a government request, with broader access planned in the coming weeks.
> This is the story of the day. Model launches now sit inside policy, access control, and geopolitical pressure. Enterprise teams should plan for staggered availability and keep fallback model paths alive.

### [Patronus AI lands $50M to build ‘digital worlds’ that stress-test AI agents](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/25/patronus-ai-lands-50m-to-build-digital-worlds-that-stress-test-ai-agents/)
Patronus AI raised $50 million to expand its agent evaluation platform. The company is building simulated environments meant to stress-test agent behavior, reliability, and failure modes before deployment.
> Evaluation keeps moving toward first-class platform spend. That makes sense. Agent systems fail in long chains, edge conditions, and weird tool states. Synthetic worlds are one of the few ways to hit that surface area before customers do.

### [What happened after 2,000 people tried to hack my AI assistant](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/26/hack-my-ai-assistant/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison highlighted Fernando Irarrázaval’s public challenge to exfiltrate secrets from an AI assistant via email. After roughly 6,000 attempts and about $500 in token spend, no one succeeded in leaking the protected secret.
> This is a useful data point for prompt-injection resilience. It does not mean the problem is solved. It does show that stronger model behavior and hard rules around tool use can raise the cost of attack in a real way.
</div>

### [AI and Liability](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/25/ai-and-liability/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison pointed to Bruce Schneier’s discussion of a German ruling that treats Google’s AI Overview output as Google’s own words for liability purposes. The broader argument is that organizations deploying AI agents should remain accountable for their mistakes.
> Legal accountability is getting sharper. That will shape enterprise architecture more than another round of leaderboard churn. Guardrails, review paths, and traceability are moving from good practice to table stakes.
