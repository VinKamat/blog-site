### [Gemma 4 Launches Apache 2.0 — Day-0 Ecosystem Support Everywhere](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-gemma-4-the-best-small-multimodal)

Google released Gemma 4 under Apache 2.0. The 26B A4B MoE model runs at 162 tok/s on an RTX 4090 at 19.5 GB VRAM; the 4B model fits on a phone. vLLM, llama.cpp, Ollama, Hugging Face, and Intel hardware all shipped support on day zero.

> The Apache license matters more than the benchmarks. Enterprise teams can now ship these models without legal review nightmares. MoE at this size is the right shape for production — 4B active parameters at 26B total is a very different cost conversation than a comparable dense model.

### [The Agent Harness Is the Real Moat](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-good-friday)

Latent Space's AINews documents developers migrating from Claude Code to Hermes Agent for long tasks, citing reliability. The edge is harness engineering: autonomous skill creation, pluggable memory backends (Honcho, mem0, Hindsight, RetainDB), inline diffs, and trace-based learning loops.

> Raw model capability is saturating fast. The teams winning at enterprise agentic deployments are investing in harness architecture — structured memory, trace collection, failure analysis. The model is increasingly just a component.

### [Eight Years of Wanting, Three Months of Building With AI](https://lalitm.com/post/building-syntaqlite-ai/)

Lalit Maganti documents building syntaqlite — a SQLite parser and formatter — with Claude Code after years of procrastination. AI accelerated the tedious grammar rules and initial prototyping. It also enabled deferring architectural decisions because refactoring felt cheap, until that debt compounded silently.

> The honest piece. For enterprise architects: AI accelerates building, it doesn't replace the design thinking that has to happen before building. The teams that skip that step are accumulating invisible technical debt at AI speed.

### [Google AI Edge Gallery: Gemma 4 Running Natively on iPhone](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Apr/6/google-ai-edge-gallery/)

Google shipped an official iOS app running Gemma 4 E2B and E4B models locally, with image Q&A, audio transcription, and tool-calling demos against eight interactive HTML widget "skills." Simon Willison notes it's the first time a vendor has shipped an official on-device model testing app.

### [AgentArmor: Open-Source 8-Layer Security Framework for AI Agents](https://github.com/Agastya910/agentarmor)

AgentArmor wraps agentic architectures with 8 security layers: prompt injection detection, vector DB encryption, instruction-data separation, action risk scoring, network egress control, PII redaction, inter-agent mutual auth, and JIT identity. Validated against all 10 OWASP ASI risks from the December 2025 spec.

> Most production agent systems have none of this. The OWASP ASI framework is the right mental model to bring into enterprise architecture reviews — particularly action risk scoring (READ=1 to ADMIN=10), which maps cleanly onto existing RBAC and least-privilege patterns.

</div>

### [Microsoft Copilot ToS: "For Entertainment Purposes Only"](https://techcrunch.com/2026/04/05/copilot-is-for-entertainment-purposes-only-according-to-microsofts-terms-of-service/)

Microsoft's Copilot terms of service classify the product as "for entertainment purposes only," joining other AI vendors whose legal language disclaim reliability even while selling enterprise deployments.

> The gap between marketing and ToS is the actual reliability posture. Enterprise AI architecture should treat the model as one input — confidence scoring, human review gates, and audit trails are the architecture. This ToS language is a reminder to build accordingly.
