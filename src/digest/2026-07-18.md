### [Claude makes Fable 5 permanent in premium plans](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/18/claude-make-fable-5-permanent/)
Anthropic said Claude Fable 5 will stay in Max and Team Premium plans starting July 20, at 50% of plan limits. Pro and Team Standard users will keep access through usage credits and will receive a one-time $100 credit.
> Subscription packaging is now a real competitive surface in AI. Teams need stable access to the best model if they are going to build workflows, training, and internal trust around it.

### [Kimi K3 raises the floor for open-weight frontier models](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/kimi-k3/)
Moonshot AI launched Kimi K3, a 2.8 trillion parameter model available through its site and API, with open weights promised by July 27. Simon Willison highlighted third-party results showing strong long-horizon knowledge-work performance and a leading position on Arena’s Frontend Code benchmark.
> This keeps pressure on every closed model vendor. Enterprise teams now have more reason to evaluate open-weight and China-based options for coding, long-context work, and cost control.

### [Firefox in WebAssembly shows how far AI-assisted engineering can stretch](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/firefox-in-webassembly/)
Puter compiled Firefox to WebAssembly so it can run inside another browser, routing traffic through a WebSocket proxy layer. The team reportedly used an estimated $25,000 worth of Claude Opus and Fable tokens during development.
> This is a good reminder that model-assisted engineering keeps pushing into strange, ambitious systems projects. The hard part shifts fast from “can we build it” to “can we operate it at scale and cost.”
</div>

### [Patreon starts actively blocking AI training crawlers](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/17/patreon-stops-asking-ai-bots-not-to-scrape-and-starts-blocking-them/)
Patreon is expanding its Cloudflare partnership to block AI training bots directly instead of relying on `robots.txt` requests. The company said tests drove some AI crawler attempts from thousands per week to zero.
> Data rights are moving out of policy docs and into enforcement infrastructure. That matters for any enterprise sitting on proprietary content, customer data, or partner IP.

### [Google AI Mode starts acting across linked apps](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/16/googles-ai-mode-now-lets-you-link-and-interact-with-select-apps/)
Google’s AI Mode can now connect to apps like Instacart, Canva, and YouTube in the U.S. and complete tasks inside those services. The feature extends AI Mode from conversational search into planning, shopping, and media workflows.
> Search is turning into an execution layer. Product teams should think hard about what parts of their app need to become callable capabilities for agents.

### [DoorDash opens a CLI for agent-driven ordering](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/16/yes-you-can-now-order-doordash-from-the-command-line/)
DoorDash launched a limited beta of `dd-cli`, a command-line tool that lets developers and AI agents search stores, build carts, and place orders. Early access is limited to U.S. and Canadian macOS developers through a waitlist.
> Agent commerce is getting real interface surface area now. Expect more vendors to expose CLIs, agent APIs, and MCP-style integrations as they compete for automation traffic.
