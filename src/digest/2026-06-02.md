### [How we contain Claude across products](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude)
Anthropic detailed how it contains Claude across `claude.ai`, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork using process sandboxes, VMs, filesystem boundaries, egress controls, and scoped approvals. The post also shared concrete failure cases, including pre-consent hook execution in Claude Code and how approval fatigue pushed the team toward auto mode and stronger OS-level sandboxing.
> This is the practical playbook for agent deployment in real environments. If your controls depend on people approving prompts forever, the system will drift. The durable pattern is containment, scoped access, and auditability.

### [Hackers Simply Asked Meta AI to Give Them Access to High-Profile Instagram Accounts. It Worked](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/1/hackers-simply-asked-meta-ai/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison highlighted reporting that attackers were able to use Meta's AI support bot to link new email addresses to target Instagram accounts and short-circuit account recovery. The core failure was simple: a conversational support path had enough authority to perform high-risk identity actions.
> This is what happens when a support flow gets wired straight into an agent without hard guardrails. Every enterprise team building AI support should keep conversational convenience far away from account recovery authority.
