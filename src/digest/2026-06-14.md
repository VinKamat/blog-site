### [As Anthropic suspends access to new models, India debates its AI future](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/13/as-anthropic-suspends-access-to-new-models-india-debates-its-ai-future/)
TechCrunch reports that Anthropic's recent model suspension has sparked debate in India over whether the country needs stronger domestic AI capacity and less dependence on foreign frontier providers. The story frames the shutdown as a policy shock with direct implications for national AI strategy.
> This is the architecture lesson hiding inside the geopolitics. If a core model can disappear on policy grounds, platform teams need multi-model routing and a cleaner portability story.

### [KPMG pulls report on AI usage due to apparent hallucinations](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/13/kpmg-pulls-report-on-ai-usage-due-to-apparent-hallucinations/)
KPMG pulled a report about AI adoption after apparent hallucinations were found in the document. The episode is another reminder that even AI analysis about AI still needs ordinary editorial controls and verification.
> This lands close to home for any enterprise AI program. Governance loses credibility fast when the reporting layer itself cannot be trusted.

### [[AINews] Benchmark reshuffle: DeepSWE replaces SWE-Bench Pro](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Latent Space highlighted Artificial Analysis replacing SWE-Bench Pro with DeepSWE in its Coding Agent Index to reduce benchmark gaming and better measure scratch-built problem solving. The change also pushed more attention toward harness quality and system design around the model.
> Agent evals are drifting toward full-stack evals. That is closer to real life, where tool loop quality, retries, and sandbox behavior shape outcomes as much as the model itself.
</div>

### [[AINews] Moonshot releases Kimi-K2.7-Code](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Moonshot released Kimi-K2.7-Code as an open coding model with reported gains over K2.6 and roughly 30% fewer reasoning tokens. Latent Space notes a 1T-parameter MoE design with 32B active parameters, 256K context, and quick support from inference tooling.
> Open models get interesting when the ecosystem moves on day one. Fast vLLM and related support means this can be tested in real pipelines instead of living as a benchmark screenshot.
</div>

### [[AINews] MiniMax M3 ships with 1M-token context](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
MiniMax released M3 as an open-weight multimodal MoE model with about 428B total parameters, around 23B active parameters, and a 1M-token context window. The feed also notes immediate support across SGLang, vLLM, Together, Baseten, Fireworks, and GGUF tooling.
> Long context only matters if deployment friction stays low. The fast infra support here makes M3 more relevant to serious evaluation than most open-weight launches.
</div>

<div class="digest-entry">
### [[AINews] Artificial Analysis launches AA-AgentPerf](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Artificial Analysis launched AA-AgentPerf, a benchmark for long-running coding agents that emphasizes production factors such as KV cache reuse, speculative decoding, and prefill/decode disaggregation. Its headline metric is Agents per Megawatt.
> This is a useful shift. Teams building agent platforms need a better cost-and-throughput lens than tokens per second, and power-normalized agent performance gets closer to that.
</div>
