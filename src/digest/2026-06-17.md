### [[AINews] GLM-5.2: the top Frontend Coding model in the world, IndexShare for Speculative Decoding](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-glm-52-the-top-frontend-coding)
Latent Space says Z.ai released GLM-5.2 as an MIT-licensed open-weight model aimed at coding and long-horizon agentic work. ### [The Fable 5 Export Controls Harm US Cyber Defense](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/16/fable-5-export-controls/)
Simon Willison pointed to Kate Moussouris’s write-up arguing that the export-control case against Anthropic’s Fable 5 centered on prompts asking the model to fix insecure code and help validate patches. Her argument is that this is standard defensive security work, and removing that capability weakens the model’s value for secure software development.
> Security teams need models that can find, fix, and test. If policy starts treating defensive coding help as suspect, enterprise AI roadmaps around secure SDLC get harder fast.

### [Probably raises $9M to build a more reliable kind of AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/16/probably-raises-9m-to-build-a-more-reliable-kind-of-ai/)
TechCrunch reports that Probably raised a $9 million seed round from Andreessen Horowitz to build AI systems that use deterministic validators, citations, and audit trails to catch hallucinations before answers reach users. The company says its harness approach lets it run smaller models while targeting much higher accuracy for precision-sensitive domains.
> This is the part of AI engineering that deserves more attention. Better harnesses, better validation, and smaller models are a sane recipe for production systems that people can actually trust.

### [ChatGPT’s market share slips below 50% for first time](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/16/chatgpts-market-share-slips-below-50-for-first-time/)
TechCrunch, citing Sensor Tower’s 2026 State of AI report, says ChatGPT fell to 46.4% market share by the end of May while Gemini rose to 27.7% and Claude reached 10.3%. OpenAI still leads in absolute scale with more than 1.1 billion monthly users, though the report says users are switching assistants more freely than before.
> The assistant market is starting to look like a portfolio decision instead of a winner-take-all one. That pushes architecture toward abstraction layers, observability, and vendor flexibility.

### [Pinterest launches an experimental AI shopping app called ‘Ask Pinterest’](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/pinterest-launches-an-experimental-ai-shopping-app-called-ask-pinterest/)
Pinterest launched a limited-access conversational shopping app called Ask Pinterest and also introduced advertiser-facing AI features, including a Pinterest Model Context Protocol layer for campaign management with third-party agentic tools. The app uses Pinterest’s Taste Graph plus saved Pins and Boards to keep context across shopping sessions.
> This is a good example of domain AI done with owned context and tight workflow boundaries. The MCP angle stands out too. More platforms are going to expose agent hooks around their proprietary data and actions.
</div>
