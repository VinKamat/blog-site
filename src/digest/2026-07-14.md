### [Directly Responsible Individuals (DRI)](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/12/directly-responsible-individuals/)
Simon Willison argued that LLM agents should never be treated as the DRI for a project, even when they do substantial work. His point is that accountability for success, failure, and decisions still belongs to a human owner.
> Teams need this line to stay sharp. Once agent output and human accountability get blurred together, governance gets sloppy and incident response gets harder.
</div>

### [Anthropic starts localizing Claude pricing for India, its biggest market after the US](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/13/anthropic-starts-localizing-claude-pricing-for-india-its-biggest-market-after-the-us/)
TechCrunch reports that Anthropic has started showing rupee-denominated Claude pricing in India, which Anthropic says is its second-largest market after the U.S. The rollout still lacks UPI support, so payment friction remains even as regional go-to-market gets more serious.
> Local pricing is product infrastructure. If model vendors want durable enterprise growth outside the U.S., procurement and billing have to feel native instead of imported.

### [Apple sues OpenAI over alleged trade secret theft](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/apple-sues-openai-over-alleged-trade-secret-theft/)
TechCrunch reports that Apple filed suit against OpenAI in the Northern District of California, alleging trade secret theft, breach of contract, and recruiting practices aimed at extracting confidential hardware information. The complaint focuses on former Apple employees now at OpenAI and ties the dispute to OpenAI's hardware ambitions.
> This is a reminder that AI competition is spilling into hardware, supply chains, and talent flows. Enterprise buyers should expect legal risk and partner risk to become part of model-vendor due diligence.
