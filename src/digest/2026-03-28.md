Google's infrastructure backing also changes the Azure/OpenAI vs. AWS/Anthropic calculus for enterprise commitments.

### [H100 Rental Prices Are Going Up — Hard](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-h100-prices-are-melting-up)
H100 GPU rental prices, which bottomed out after the DeepSeek R1 shock in early 2025, have reversed sharply since December 2025. Multiple sources suggest H100s are now worth *more* than they were three years ago. The combination of reasoning model demand, agentic workloads, and better inference software has made a 4-year-old chip more valuable than original depreciation schedules assumed.

> This is the part of the AI infrastructure story that doesn't get enough attention. The assumption that GPU prices would follow traditional semiconductor cycles was wrong. Reasoning models and multi-agent workflows are far more compute-intensive than basic inference — and enterprise adoption is accelerating right when supply is constrained. For anyone managing cloud AI budgets: if you locked in reserved compute at 2025 lows, that's looking smart. If you didn't, you're negotiating against a tighter market.

### [We Rewrote JSONata with AI in a Day, Saved $500K/Year](https://www.reco.ai/blog/we-rewrote-jsonata-with-ai)
The Reco team used AI-assisted vibe porting to build a custom Go implementation of the JSONata JSON expression language in 7 hours for $400 in token spend. The key enabler was JSONata's existing test suite. They ran a shadow deployment in parallel for a week before switching over. Via [Simon Willison](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/27/vine-porting-jsonata/).

> The shadow deployment discipline here is what separates this from a stunt. Any enterprise considering AI-assisted rewrites needs a parallel-run verification strategy — and ideally a comprehensive test suite before they start, not after. The $400/$500K ratio is striking, but the real cost was the engineering judgment to know when to trust the output. That judgment doesn't come cheap and it doesn't come from the AI.

### [Vibe Coding SwiftUI Apps with Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Mar/27/vibe-coding-swiftui/)
Simon Willison built two macOS menu-bar utility apps — Bandwidther (per-app network bandwidth) and Gpuer (GPU/RAM monitoring) — using Claude Opus 4.6 and GPT-5.4 without opening Xcode or knowing Swift. A full SwiftUI app fits in a single file, which removes the friction that normally makes native app development expensive.

> The single-file SwiftUI constraint is exactly the kind of affordance that makes agentic coding tractable — no project scaffolding, no build system to fight, no state to maintain across files. This is a preview of what enterprise tool development will look like when teams can spin up a native UI wrapper for an internal API in an afternoon. The caveat Simon raises is real: vibe-coded apps measuring system internals may be confidently wrong. Verification still requires domain knowledge the AI doesn't have.

### [OpenAI Shuts Down Sora](https://techcrunch.com/video/openai-shuts-down-sora-while-meta-gets-shut-out-in-court/)
OpenAI is shutting down Sora, its AI video generation product, as part of a broader pattern of axing side projects this week. This comes alongside a TechCrunch analysis suggesting SoftBank's new $40B loan from JPMorgan and Goldman Sachs is setting up a 2026 OpenAI IPO.

> Sora launched with significant fanfare as a proof of OpenAI's multimodal ambitions, but video generation never got traction as a product — the gap between demo quality and reliable output quality remained wide. The real signal here is OpenAI's increasing focus on the core AI assistant and agentic product lines ahead of a potential IPO. Investors want clean unit economics, and a loss-making video product doesn't help the story. The SoftBank loan structure suggests the IPO is more likely than not this year.

A federal judge ordered the Trump administration to rescind recent restrictions it placed on Anthropic in connection with the Defense Department. David Sacks, who served as White House AI czar, has also stepped down from that role.

> The policy landscape around enterprise AI and defense is moving fast and unpredictably. Anthropic winning an injunction matters less as a legal story and more as a signal that AI labs are now willing to push back on executive overreach through the courts. For enterprise architects working on government or defense adjacent workloads, the regulatory environment is less stable than it appeared six months ago — and vendor relationships with AI labs carry policy risk that didn't exist before.

### [Mistral Releases Open-Source Speech Generation Model](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/mistral-releases-a-new-open-source-model-for-speech-generation/)
Mistral launched an open-source voice model targeting sales and customer engagement use cases, putting it in direct competition with ElevenLabs, Deepgram, and OpenAI. On the same day, Cohere released a 2B-parameter open-source transcription model supporting 14 languages, runnable on consumer GPUs for self-hosting.

> Two enterprise-grade voice models in one day, both open-source, both positioned for self-hosting. The commoditization of voice AI at the model layer is accelerating faster than the application layer can absorb it. For conversational AI platforms — including customer service and contact center applications — this changes the build-vs-buy calculation significantly. The question is no longer whether you can afford good voice AI; it's whether your orchestration and integration layers are ready to take advantage of it.
