### [The new GPT-5.6 family: Luna, Terra, Sol](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/9/gpt-5-6/#atom-everything)
OpenAI released GPT-5.6 in three sizes, with a 1 million token context window, up to 128,000 output tokens, and new API features including programmatic tool calling, multi-agent orchestration, prompt cache breakpoints, and original-detail image handling. Simon Willison also highlighted OpenAI's benchmark claims around long-running agentic workflows and the pricing spread across the three tiers.
> This turns model selection into an architecture concern. Teams need routing rules, budget controls, and evals that show when Luna is enough and when Sol earns the spend.

### [Introducing Muse Spark 1.1](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/9/muse-spark-1-1/#atom-everything)
Meta's Muse Spark 1.1 is the first Spark model with an API, and Meta is pitching stronger agentic tool calling and computer use. Simon Willison points to the evaluation report and notes he already published a CLI plugin to test the model directly.
> Meta is aiming at the workflow layer. If Spark holds up on tool use and computer use, it becomes relevant for migrations, ops assistants, and internal engineering copilots.

### [OpenAI is shutting down Atlas, but its AI browser ambitions are still growing](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/09/openai-is-shutting-down-atlas-but-its-ai-browser-ambitions-are-still-growing/)
TechCrunch says OpenAI is sunsetting Atlas less than a year after launch while moving some agentic browsing features into its desktop app and a Chrome extension. The company is narrowing the product surface while keeping browser automation in play.
> Browser automation is settling into a platform capability. The important design question is where approvals, session state, and audit trails live once browsing moves inside desktop agents.

### [Hugging Face’s CEO on why companies are done renting their AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/10/hugging-faces-ceo-on-why-companies-are-done-renting-their-ai/)
Hugging Face CEO Clem Delangue argues that open source AI is accelerating and says the company's models and datasets are now used by roughly half of the Fortune 500. The piece frames enterprise demand around control, cost, and the ability to avoid full dependence on closed vendors.
