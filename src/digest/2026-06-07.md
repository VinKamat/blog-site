### [OpenAI unveils Lockdown Mode to protect sensitive data from prompt injection attacks](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/06/openai-unveils-lockdown-mode-to-protect-sensitive-data-from-prompt-injection-attacks/)
OpenAI rolled out Lockdown Mode for ChatGPT Business and eligible personal accounts. It disables live browsing, web images, deep research, and agent mode to reduce prompt injection and data exfiltration risk.
> This is a real enterprise feature, not a demo checkbox. Teams are finally treating prompt injection as an architecture problem, and the control surface now matters as much as the model.

### [Microsoft's new MAI models](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/2/microsofts-new-models/#atom-everything)
Microsoft introduced MAI-Thinking-1 and MAI-Code-1-Flash, pushing its in-house model line further into reasoning and coding. Simon Willison's write-up highlights the scale, the active-parameter footprint, and Microsoft's claim that the models were built end to end in-house.
> Microsoft is building a full stack here, model, platform, and distribution through Copilot and VS Code. For enterprise buyers, that matters more than leaderboard chatter because it points to tighter product integration and stronger governance hooks.

### [Google will pay SpaceX $920M per month for compute](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/google-will-pay-spacex-920m-per-month-for-compute/)
Google agreed to pay SpaceX $920 million per month from October 2026 through June 2029 for access to about 110,000 NVIDIA GPUs, CPUs, memory, and related components. Google said the deal is short-term bridge capacity driven by higher-than-expected demand for its agent platform and Gemini Enterprise.
> This is the clearest signal that AI capacity planning is still broken at the high end. Even hyperscalers are scrambling for bridge compute, which means enterprise pricing pressure is not easing anytime soon.
</div>

### [The token bill comes due: Inside the industry scramble to manage AI’s runaway costs](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/the-token-bill-comes-due-inside-the-industry-scramble-to-manage-ais-runaway-costs/)
TechCrunch reports that companies are blowing through AI budgets as agentic tools drive far higher token consumption, even as per-token prices fall. The Linux Foundation is launching the Tokenomics Foundation to bring FinOps-style discipline and standards to AI spend tracking.
> This is where the next enterprise AI wave gets decided. The winners will be the teams that can connect token burn to shipped value, audit it clearly, and shut down waste before finance does it for them.
</div>

<div class="digest-entry">
### [How to Stop Shipping Low-Quality RL Environments (with Examples)](https://www.latent.space/p/bad-envs)
Auriel Wright argues that brittle RL training harnesses poison model learning by generating bad trajectories, stale state, reward hacking, and unrealistic mock data. The piece frames environment quality as a software engineering discipline, not just a research concern.
> This hits a nerve because a weak harness can teach the wrong behavior at scale. Anyone building agents should read this as a warning that eval and training infrastructure quality is now core product quality.
</div>

<div class="digest-entry">
### [Reality: The Final Eval — Lukas Petersson and Axel Backlund of Andon Labs](https://www.latent.space/p/andon)
Latent Space digs into Andon Labs' real-world agent evaluations, including Vending-Bench, AI-run retail operations, and long-horizon tests where models manage money, tools, customers, and employees. The discussion shows how agents behave once benchmarks give way to real incentives, physical constraints, and messy human interaction.
> This is the direction enterprise evals need to move. If an agent cannot survive real workflows, real users, and long time horizons, the benchmark score is trivia.
</div>
