### [What's new in Claude Sonnet 5](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/30/claude-sonnet-5/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison highlighted Anthropic's developer notes for Claude Sonnet 5: a 1 million token context window, 128,000 max output tokens, adaptive thinking enabled by default, and removal of `temperature`, `top_p`, and `top_k`. He also flagged that nominal pricing stayed the same while the new tokenizer produces roughly 30% more tokens for English text.
> The context window changes what you can keep inside one call. The tokenizer change matters just as much, because cost models and latency assumptions will shift the minute teams swap this into production.

### [Cloudflare's new policy pushes AI companies to pay for publishers' content](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/01/cloudflares-new-policy-pushes-ai-companies-to-pay-for-publishers-content/)
Cloudflare is giving AI companies until September 15 to separate crawlers used for search from crawlers used for AI training and agents. Publishers will be able to block AI access by default unless those distinctions and payment paths are in place.
> This lands directly on RAG and agent systems that treat the open web as a free data plane. Content rights are becoming an architecture constraint, and teams need cleaner provenance and licensing controls.
</div>

### [Gemini Spark, Google's agentic assistant, is now available on Mac](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/01/gemini-spark-googles-agentic-assistant-is-now-available-on-mac/)
Google brought Gemini Spark to Mac with real-time tracking and broader app support. The product keeps pushing the assistant from a browser tab into the desktop layer where everyday work actually happens.
> Desktop agents get interesting when they can watch state and move across apps. The real work is permission design, recovery, and making partial execution visible to the person using it.

### [Meta, like SpaceX, looks to turn excess AI compute into cash](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/01/meta-like-spacex-looks-to-turn-excess-ai-compute-into-cash/)
TechCrunch reports that Meta is developing plans to sell access to AI compute power and models as a cloud infrastructure business. That would put Meta into more direct competition with AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure.
> The model race is bleeding into the infrastructure market. Buyers will get more supply options, and they will also face a harder set of platform and lock-in choices.
