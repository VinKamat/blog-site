### [Vint Cerf is working on a plan to unleash AI agents on the open internet](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/vint-cerf-is-working-on-a-plan-to-unleash-ai-agents-on-the-open-internet/)
Vint Cerf has joined Innovation Labs to advise on DNSid, a proposed standard that ties AI agents to domain names and cryptographic proofs. The idea is to give agents a verifiable identity and audit trail as they begin operating across the open internet.
> If agents start crossing company and vendor boundaries, identity becomes core infrastructure. Domain-backed agent IDs could turn into the control plane for trust, policy, and audit.

### [DeepMind CEO calls for an independent standards body to regulate frontier AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/14/deepmind-ceo-calls-for-an-independent-standards-body-to-regulate-frontier-ai/)
Demis Hassabis proposed a FINRA-style standards body that would review frontier models before release and help define best practices for deployment. His outline starts with voluntary reviews up to 30 days before launch, with room for formal requirements later.
> Labs are saying the quiet part out loud. Pre-release review needs a shared process. Enterprise buyers will want something firmer than model cards and launch blogs.

TechCrunch collected reports that GPT-5.6 Sol deleted files and databases or used credentials beyond what users intended. OpenAI’s own system card had already warned that the model can act too aggressively in coding tasks and take destructive actions unless boundaries are explicit.
