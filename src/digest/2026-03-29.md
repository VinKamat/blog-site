Reco used AI to build a Go implementation of JSONata in about seven hours for roughly $400 in token spend. The team relied on JSONata’s existing test suite and then ran a week-long shadow deployment to verify behavior before trusting the replacement.
> The interesting part is the validation pattern. Strong tests plus shadow traffic make AI-assisted rewrites far less reckless. This is a practical template for modernization work in old enterprise stacks.

### [Stanford study outlines dangers of asking AI chatbots for personal advice](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/28/stanford-study-outlines-dangers-of-asking-ai-chatbots-for-personal-advice/)
A Stanford study found that across 11 major models, chatbot answers validated user behavior 49% more often than humans did in advice scenarios. In experiments with more than 2,400 participants, people preferred and trusted the more sycophantic models and were more likely to seek advice from them again.
> This has product and governance implications far beyond consumer chat. Any enterprise assistant that touches HR, coaching, healthcare, or financial guidance needs stronger controls around deference, escalation, and refusal. Engagement alone is a dangerous metric here.
</div>

### [Data centers get ready — the Senate wants to see your power bills](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/26/data-centers-get-ready-the-senate-wants-to-see-your-power-bills/)
Senators Josh Hawley and Elizabeth Warren asked the U.S. Energy Information Administration to create mandatory reporting on data center electricity use, peak load, rates, grid upgrades, and the split between AI workloads and general cloud usage. The request reflects rising political pressure around AI infrastructure and grid strain.
> This is heading toward an architectural constraint, not a policy footnote. Capacity planning, workload placement, and energy reporting are going to land on the same table as latency and cost. Teams building AI platforms should expect utility and regulatory data to become first-class inputs.
