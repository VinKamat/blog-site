### [Datasette Apps: Host custom HTML applications inside Datasette](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/18/datasette-apps/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison launched `datasette-apps` on June 18, a plugin for running self-contained HTML and JavaScript apps inside a tightly sandboxed iframe on top of Datasette. The apps can issue read-only SQL by default, with writes gated through stored queries and additional permission controls.
> This is a strong pattern for internal AI tools. Generated UI lives in a sandbox, data access stays on an allow-list, and every query can be inspected. That is the kind of shape enterprise teams can actually work with.

Z.ai released the open weights for GLM-5.2 on June 16 under an MIT license. Simon Willison highlights its 1 million token context window, leading placement on the Artificial Analysis open-weights index, and a No. 2 spot on the Code Arena WebDev leaderboard.
> Open models keep getting more credible for serious coding and long-context work. When a strong model is open and broadly hosted, architecture teams get more freedom on placement, resilience, and compliance design.

### [The Fable 5 Export Controls Harm US Cyber Defense](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/16/fable-5-export-controls/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison points to Kate Moussouris arguing that the prompt behind the Fable 5 restrictions was essentially a defensive coding request: review vulnerable code, fix it, and verify the patch. Her argument is that patching and validation are core security uses, not a guardrail bypass.
> This is a policy line worth watching closely. If regulators treat patching help as offensive capability, they will make secure SDLC work slower and weaker. Teams need a much clearer way to describe defensive AI use to risk and policy groups.
</div>

### [Nobel laureate John Jumper is leaving DeepMind for rival Anthropic](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/20/nobel-laureate-john-jumper-is-leaving-deepmind-for-rival-anthropic/)
John Jumper announced on June 20 that he is leaving Google DeepMind for Anthropic after nearly nine years there. TechCrunch reports that he had been a key member of Google’s coding-tools effort and follows other recent high-profile moves out of DeepMind.
> Talent moves at this level usually mark where serious momentum is building. It also says the coding-agent race is strategic enough to pull in researchers with real scientific weight.

### [Source: Elastic agrees to buy CRV-backed Deductive AI for up to $85M](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/18/source-elastic-agrees-to-buy-crv-backed-deductiveai-for-up-to-85m/)
TechCrunch reports that Elastic has agreed to buy Deductive AI for up to $85 million. Deductive was founded in 2023, built AI SRE agents for catching and resolving software bugs, and had reached roughly $1 million in ARR according to the report.
> This is a familiar enterprise move now. Incumbents are buying focused agent startups and folding them into platforms customers already trust. Observability plus AI remediation is a practical wedge because the signal, urgency, and ROI are all easy to see.

### [Billionaire Ambani wants AI in every call, app, and home](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/19/billionaire-ambani-wants-ai-in-every-call-app-and-home/)
Reliance announced Jio Call Agent, an AI-enabled MyJio app, and a home device called TeleFrame, pushing AI features into calls, mobile workflows, and connected-home surfaces for Jio’s more than 500 million users. The company also previewed AI services for healthcare, education, agriculture, and small businesses, including multilingual support.
> Distribution changes everything here. When AI ships inside the telecom layer and default consumer surfaces, adoption can move fast. The governance questions show up just as fast, especially around consent, retention, and model-training use of customer data.
