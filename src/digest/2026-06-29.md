### [Quoting Jon Udell](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/28/jon-udell/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison highlighted Jon Udell's argument that agent-assisted software development should stay inside human-owned review loops. The point is simple: agents can join the team, but they should not dump opaque work products on it.
> This lines up with real engineering practice. If an agent leaves behind a giant unreadable diff, the speed is fake and the maintenance bill shows up later.

### [What happened after 2,000 people tried to hack my AI assistant](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/26/hack-my-ai-assistant/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison covered Fernando Irarrázaval's public challenge to exfiltrate secrets from an AI assistant via email. After roughly 6,000 attempts and about $500 in token spend, nobody managed to leak the protected secret.
> This is a useful signal on prompt-injection hardening. It still leaves the same enterprise lesson: keep tools least-privileged, keep approvals tight, and assume a determined attacker will keep probing.
</div>
