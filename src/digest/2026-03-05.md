### [Agentic Engineering Anti-patterns](https://simonwillison.net/guides/agentic-engineering-patterns/anti-patterns/)

Simon Willison adds a new chapter to his Agentic Engineering Patterns guide: don't file PRs with AI-generated code you haven't personally reviewed and tested. The pattern is rampant and shifts cognitive load to reviewers rather than the person who prompted the agent.

> This is a process engineering problem, not just an individual behavior one. Enterprises scaling AI-assisted development need explicit review standards — evidence of testing, explanation of implementation choices, small scoped PRs — baked into their Definition of Done. Culture policies need to move as fast as the tooling does.

### [Claude Code Gets Voice Mode](https://techcrunch.com/2026/03/03/claude-code-rolls-out-a-voice-mode-capability/)

Anthropic rolled out voice interaction in Claude Code, letting developers speak commands and queries to their coding agent instead of typing them.

> Voice is a modest UX win for developer flow, but it surfaces a deeper architectural challenge: spoken intent is far more ambiguous than typed instructions, and in agentic loops where one misunderstood command can cascade through a codebase, ambiguity resolution needs to be a first-class concern — not an afterthought.

The Information reports GPT-5.4 is coming with a ~1 million token context window and a new "extreme reasoning mode" that can deliberate for hours on long-horizon agentic tasks. OpenAI is also reportedly shifting to monthly model update cadences. All unconfirmed.

> A 1M context window fundamentally reshapes RAG architecture decisions — when you can load entire knowledge domains into context, the tradeoffs between retrieval precision and context stuffing change dramatically. The "hours of reasoning" framing is the more interesting signal for enterprise: high-stakes workflows like compliance review, financial analysis, or risk modeling may actually benefit from slower, deliberate reasoning over fast responses.
