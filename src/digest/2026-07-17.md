### [Kimi K3, and what we can still learn from the pelican benchmark](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/16/kimi-k3/)

Moonshot AI launched Kimi K3 on July 16, 2026 as a 2.8 trillion parameter model available via its website and API, with open weights promised by July 27, 2026. Simon Willison highlighted early benchmark claims that place it near the top of the field on long-horizon knowledge work and frontend coding, with pricing at $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens.

> Open models keep climbing into serious enterprise territory. The weight release matters more than the benchmark chest-thumping because teams can actually test cost, latency, and control in their own stack.

### [Thinking Machines amps up its bet against one-size-fits-all AI with its first open model, Inkling](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/thinking-machines-amps-up-its-bet-against-one-size-fits-all-ai-with-its-first-open-model-inkling/)

Thinking Machines Lab released Inkling, its first open-weight in-house model, on July 15, 2026. TechCrunch reports it is a 975B-parameter mixture-of-experts model with about 41B active parameters per task, trained on 45T multimodal tokens, and aimed at organizations that want to fine-tune and adapt the model themselves.

> This is aimed squarely at companies that want to shape the model themselves. That lines up with where production AI is headed: a smaller number of premium frontier calls, and a larger volume of private or customized open models.

### [Google’s AI Mode now lets you link and interact with select apps](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/16/googles-ai-mode-now-lets-you-link-and-interact-with-select-apps/)

Google announced on July 16, 2026 that AI Mode can now connect to apps including Instacart, Canva, and YouTube in the U.S. The new integrations let users move from asking questions to completing tasks such as building grocery carts, browsing design templates, and saving playlists.

> Search is turning into an action layer. The architecture question is identity, permissions, and transaction safety once these connectors move from demos to daily workflow.

### [Yes, you can now order DoorDash from the command line](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/16/yes-you-can-now-order-doordash-from-the-command-line/)

DoorDash opened a limited beta for `dd-cli` on July 16, 2026, giving U.S. and Canadian macOS developers a command-line tool that lets AI agents search stores, find deals, and complete checkout. TechCrunch frames it as an early example of agentic commerce exposed directly to software.

> This looks goofy until you see the pattern. More consumer platforms are exposing agent-facing interfaces, and the winners will be the ones with clean ordering, payment, and trust boundaries.

### [Why the first GPU financiers are turning to inference chips in a $400 million deal](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/17/why-the-first-gpu-financiers-are-turning-to-inference-chips-in-a-400-million-deal/)

General Compute secured a $400 million loan from Upper90 backed by inference-focused chips, according to TechCrunch on July 17, 2026. The deal points to rising demand for cheaper open-model inference infrastructure built around alternatives to Nvidia GPUs, including SambaNova silicon.

> This is an infrastructure market signal. If capital starts favoring inference economics over training glamour, enterprise AI costs should get easier to defend.

### [Applied Computing wants to give oil and gas operators an AI model for the entire plant](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/applied-computing-wants-to-give-oil-and-gas-operators-an-ai-model-for-the-entire-plant/)

Applied Computing raised a $20 million Series A led by KBR, with Databricks Ventures participating, to scale Orbital, its plant-wide industrial AI model. TechCrunch says the system combines time-series data, physics-based modeling, and language interfaces to detect anomalies, trace causes, and simulate operational changes across oil, gas, and petrochemical facilities.

> Vertical AI gets real when it blends domain physics with language interfaces. This is closer to a useful industrial copilot than a generic chatbot dropped on top of sensor data.
