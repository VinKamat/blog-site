### [[AINews] Thinky's Inkling: 975B-A41B multimodal, new best American Apache 2.0 open model (with Inkling-Small, 276B-A12B)](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-thinkys-inkling-975b-a41b)

The release also includes a smaller Inkling-Small preview and day-one support across tools like vLLM, SGLang, Hugging Face, Baseten, Modal, and Databricks.
> Open multimodal models get more interesting when the ecosystem is ready on day one. This gives enterprise teams a tunable base model with fewer platform headaches and a clearer path to private deployment.

### [xai-org/grok-build, now open source](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/15/grok-build/)
xAI open-sourced its Grok Build coding agent after backlash over a behavior that uploaded whole directories to Google Cloud buckets, including sensitive local files in at least one reported case. xAI says it disabled retention by default on July 12 and is deleting previously retained coding data.
> This is a trust story. Agent harnesses now sit close to source code, secrets, and local state, so data flow and retention policy have become product features.

### [How I tricked Claude into leaking your deepest, darkest secrets](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/15/claude-web-fetch-exfiltration/)
Simon Willison highlighted Ayush Paul’s report on a Claude `web_fetch` loophole that let an attacker exfiltrate user details by chaining through links embedded in previously fetched pages. Anthropic has since closed the gap by removing that follow-on navigation path.
> Tool safety lives in the edges. If an agent can browse, remember, and follow instructions across steps, every navigation rule matters.

### [Anthropic, Blackstone bet the next trillion-dollar AI business is implementation, not just models](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/anthropic-blackstone-bet-the-next-trillion-dollar-ai-business-is-implementation-not-models/)

### [Applied Computing wants to give oil and gas operators an AI model for the entire plant](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/applied-computing-wants-to-give-oil-and-gas-operators-an-ai-model-for-the-entire-plant/)
Applied Computing raised a $20 million Series A led by KBR to scale Orbital, its industrial AI model for oil, gas, and petrochemical facilities. The company says Orbital combines time-series modeling, physics, chemistry, and language reasoning to predict plant state and simulate operational changes.
> This is the kind of vertical AI story that feels real. When domain models fuse physical systems with language interfaces, they start looking like decision support systems people will actually trust.

### [Microsoft patches record number of security vulnerabilities, citing its use of AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/15/microsoft-patches-record-number-of-security-vulnerabilities-citing-its-use-of-ai/)
Microsoft issued fixes for 570 vulnerabilities across Windows, Office, and other products in its July Patch Tuesday release, including two actively exploited zero-days. The company said higher patch volume is a result of using AI to uncover more flaws in its codebase.
> AI-assisted discovery is going to increase the patch load for everyone. Security teams will need better prioritization and rollout discipline as model-driven finding keeps speeding up.
