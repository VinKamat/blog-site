### [Running Python code in a sandbox with MicroPython and WASM](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/6/micropython-in-a-sandbox/)
Simon Willison published `micropython-wasm`, an alpha package that runs Python inside WebAssembly using Wasmtime. The project focuses on controlled file access, network restrictions, memory limits, CPU fuel limits, and host-function hooks for agent-style plugin execution.
> This matters because agent platforms keep tripping over code execution risk. A lightweight sandbox with explicit host boundaries is the kind of primitive that makes tool use safer and easier to ship.

### [How to Stop Shipping Low-Quality RL Environments (with Examples)](https://www.latent.space/p/bad-envs)
Auriel Wright argues that weak RL harnesses poison model training by generating bad trajectories, stale state, broken rewards, and unrealistic mock data. The piece frames environment quality as a software engineering problem that directly affects model behavior in production.
> This is the right lens for agent training. Teams obsess over the model and ignore the harness, then wonder why the system learns shortcuts, reward hacks, and brittle workflows.
</div>

### [Google will pay SpaceX $920M per month for compute](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/google-will-pay-spacex-920m-per-month-for-compute/)
Google disclosed a deal to pay SpaceX $920 million per month from October 2026 through June 2029 for access to roughly 110,000 NVIDIA GPUs, CPUs, memory, and related components. Google said the agreement is bridge capacity to handle stronger-than-expected demand for its AI products, including its agent platform and Gemini Enterprise.
> The compute story keeps getting more extreme. If hyperscalers are renting emergency capacity at this scale, enterprise teams should assume model access, pricing, and latency will stay tied to infrastructure bottlenecks for a while.
</div>

### [The token bill comes due: Inside the industry scramble to manage AI’s runaway costs](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/the-token-bill-comes-due-inside-the-industry-scramble-to-manage-ais-runaway-costs/)
TechCrunch reports that enterprise AI bills are spiking as agentic tools drive far higher token consumption, even while per-token prices fall. The Linux Foundation is launching the Tokenomics Foundation to standardize AI cost tracking, billing definitions, and usage metrics.
> Cost governance is moving from side issue to first-class architecture concern. Routing, observability, budget controls, and task-to-model fit now belong in the core design, not in cleanup work after launch.

### [Apple approves Poke as the first AI agent on its Messages for Business platform](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/04/apple-approves-poke-as-the-first-ai-agent-on-its-messages-for-business-platform/)
Poke became the first stand-alone AI agent approved for Apple’s Messages for Business platform, extending its text-based agent from SMS, Telegram, and WhatsApp to iMessage. The approval required live-support capability, clear agent labeling, and interface changes to satisfy Apple’s messaging rules.
> This is a useful distribution signal. Messaging surfaces are turning into agent runtimes, and the gatekeepers will shape trust, UX constraints, and platform economics as much as the models do.
</div>

### [AirTrunk commits $30B to build 5GW of AI data centers in India](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/05/airtrunk-commits-30b-to-build-5gw-of-ai-data-centers-in-india/)
AirTrunk said it will invest $30 billion in India by 2030 to develop 5 gigawatts of new data center capacity. The move adds to a larger wave of AI infrastructure expansion in India, helped by tax incentives, technical talent, and government support for export-oriented cloud workloads.
> This is one more sign that AI infrastructure is going multi-region faster than expected. India is shaping up as a serious capacity hub, which matters for latency, sovereignty, and cost planning across global AI systems.
</div>
