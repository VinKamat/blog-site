### [Gemini 3.5 Flash: more expensive, but Google plan to use it for everything](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/19/gemini-35-flash/)
Simon Willison notes that Google shipped Gemini 3.5 Flash straight to general availability on May 19 and is rolling it across Search, the Gemini app, AI Studio, Android Studio, and enterprise products. He also points out a steep price increase, with published pricing of $1.50 per million input tokens and $9 per million output tokens, plus a beta Interactions API for server-side conversation history.
> Wide rollout tells you Google trusts this model operationally. The price jump tells you the cheap-model era is getting thinner, so architecture discipline around routing, caching, and workload tiering matters more than ever.

### [How to use Google’s new AI agents to go beyond your standard searches](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/how-to-use-googles-new-ai-agents-to-go-beyond-your-standard-searches/)
TechCrunch reports that Google introduced “information agents” in Search at I/O 2026. These agents can monitor topics in the background, synthesize updates, and push notifications for tracked subjects, with a U.S. rollout to Google AI Pro and Ultra subscribers planned for summer 2026.
> This is Google Alerts rebuilt for the agent era. If it works well, teams will expect software to keep watching and summarizing instead of waiting for manual prompts.

### [Agentic app coding gets an upgrade with Google’s release of Android CLI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/agentic-app-coding-gets-an-upgrade-with-googles-release-of-android-cli/)
Google says Android CLI is now stable at version 1.0 and can be used by AI agents such as Claude Code, Codex, Gemini, and Antigravity to build Android apps. The tool exposes Android Studio knowledge and commands through the command line so external coding agents can use platform-specific context during development.
> This is a real platform move. The winners in agentic coding will be the ecosystems that expose their domain knowledge cleanly, instead of forcing every agent to rediscover platform rules from docs and trial-and-error.

### [OpenAI is making it easier to check if an image was made by their models](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/19/openai-is-making-it-easier-to-check-if-an-image-was-made-by-their-models/)
OpenAI announced two provenance measures for AI-generated images on May 19: support for the C2PA metadata standard and Google’s SynthID invisible watermark. The company is also previewing a public verification tool that can check for those signals in images generated by OpenAI products.
> Provenance is turning into infrastructure. Anyone building AI into customer workflows should pay attention, because trust controls are starting to matter as much as model quality.
