### [GLM-5.2 is probably the most powerful text-only open weights LLM](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/17/glm-52/)
Z.ai released GLM-5.2 open weights on June 16 after a June 13 subscriber rollout. The 753B-parameter MoE model has 40 active parameters, a 1 million token context window, and now leads the Artificial Analysis open-weights index, though it uses more output tokens than rival models.
> Open-weight competition is moving fast again. The model is strong, but the real question is whether teams can absorb its token appetite and turn that into usable enterprise throughput.

### [[AINews] Midjourney Medical: scan your organs like you step on a scale](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-midjourney-medical-scan-your)
Midjourney unveiled a prototype whole-body ultrasonic CT system called the Midjourney Scanner, plus plans for a consumer-facing spa in San Francisco by the end of 2027. The company says the current Gen 1 system uses 358,000 ultrasonic elements, reconstructs scans across 21 servers, and is in early FDA discussions.
> This is a weird and serious bet. It points to a bigger pattern: AI companies are starting to fund new data and sensing infrastructure, because model quality will increasingly depend on who owns the pipeline from the physical world.

### [World leaders want American AI. They just don’t want America to be able to turn it off.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/world-leaders-want-american-ai-they-just-dont-want-america-to-be-able-to-turn-it-off/)
At the June 17 G7 summit, Emmanuel Macron and Narendra Modi raised concerns that countries building on U.S. AI providers could lose access overnight. The debate intensified after the Trump administration blocked Anthropic from exporting its newest Mythos 5 and Fable 5 models on national security grounds.
> This will land directly in architecture reviews. Any serious international AI platform now needs portability, provider abstraction, and a plan for policy-driven shutdowns.
</div>

### [Pramaana Labs raises $27M seed round from Khosla Ventures to bring formal verification to AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/pramaana-labs-raises-27-million-seed-round-from-khosla-ventures-to-bring-formal-verification-to-ai/)
Pramaana Labs announced a $27 million seed round led by Khosla Ventures to build AI systems for high-risk fields such as law, drug discovery, and tax preparation. Its approach combines a conventional LLM with a deterministic verification layer inspired by formal methods and the LEAN proof ecosystem.
> This is one of the more credible enterprise patterns in the market. Prompting alone will not carry regulated workflows, and verification layers are finally getting real funding.
</div>

### [Collecting robot training data is dirty, unglamorous work. Some AI labs are already paying XDOF to do it.](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/collecting-robot-training-data-is-dirty-unglamorous-work-some-ai-labs-are-already-paying-xdof-to-do-it/)
XDOF emerged from stealth with $70 million in backing to build robotics data collection, cleaning, and annotation infrastructure. The company says it already works with 20 customers, including frontier labs, and is helping release a Berkeley-linked dataset with 130,000 robot manipulation trajectories.
> Physical AI has the same upstream problem LLMs once had: data scarcity. Whoever builds the best feedback loop for real-world robot data could end up owning a very valuable layer of the stack.
</div>

### [World model maker Odyssey nabs $1.45B valuation backed by Amazon and other big names](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/17/world-model-maker-odyssey-nabs-1-45b-valuation-backed-by-amazon-and-other-big-names/)
Odyssey raised a $310 million Series B at a $1.45 billion valuation, with Amazon, AMD Ventures, GV, and others participating. The startup says AWS is now its preferred cloud provider and plans to optimize its world models for Trainium chips.
> The funding is big, but the infrastructure alignment is the more interesting signal. When model companies start locking into cloud and silicon partnerships this early, platform gravity starts shaping the roadmap.
</div>
