### [[AINews] not much happened today](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-not-much-happened-today-830)
Latent Space’s July 18 roundup says Moonshot’s Kimi K3 is now forcing a serious re-rating of Chinese open-weight models, with strong coding and agent benchmarks and heavy discussion around Kimi Delta Attention, inference efficiency, and harness design. ### [Databricks hits $188B valuation, extending its run as AI’s favorite second act](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/17/databricks-hits-188b-valuation-extending-its-run-as-ais-favorite-second-act/)

### [Why the first GPU financiers are turning to inference chips in a $400 million deal](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/17/why-the-first-gpu-financiers-are-turning-to-inference-chips-in-a-400-million-deal/)
TechCrunch says General Compute secured a $400 million loan backed by inference-focused chips, a structure framed as one of the first major financings to use inference silicon as collateral. The company is betting that cheaper, power-efficient alternatives to Nvidia GPUs will matter more as open models push demand toward cost-sensitive serving.
> This is where the money starts telling the truth. Once financing shifts from training GPUs to inference hardware, everyone is signaling that production economics matter more than lab theater.
</div>

### [Patreon stops asking AI bots not to scrape — and starts blocking them](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/17/patreon-stops-asking-ai-bots-not-to-scrape-and-starts-blocking-them/)
TechCrunch reports that Patreon expanded its Cloudflare integration to actively block AI training crawlers instead of relying on `robots.txt` requests. Patreon says test results took weekly access attempts from thousands to zero for individual training bots while still allowing search-style indexing bots.
> Expect more platforms to move from policy language to technical enforcement. Consent is becoming an infrastructure feature.
