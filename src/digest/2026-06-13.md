### [Anthropic’s safety warnings may have just backfired — the government has pulled the plug on its most powerful AI](https://techcrunch.com/2026/06/12/anthropics-safety-warnings-may-have-just-backfired-the-government-has-pulled-the-plug-on-its-most-powerful-ai/)
The U.S. government ordered Anthropic to shut off Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 on June 12, 2026, citing national security concerns. Anthropic says the order was tied to a claimed jailbreak and says similar capability is already available in other public frontier models.
> This is the cleanest recent example of model dependency risk. If your product leans on a single frontier API, policy and export controls can become an uptime problem overnight.

### [OpenAI WebRTC Audio Session, now with document context](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jun/12/openai-webrtc/#atom-everything)
Simon Willison updated his OpenAI WebRTC audio demo to use GPT-Realtime-2 and added the ability to paste large document context into a live browser voice session. The result is a lightweight way to talk through source material in real time.
> Voice starts to matter once it can reason over the same working context as text chat. This points toward review, support, and analyst workflows where spoken interaction saves time instead of just feeling novel.

### [[AINews] Fable and Mythos officially too dangerous to release](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Artificial Analysis replaced SWE-Bench Pro with DeepSWE in its Coding Agent Index to reduce benchmark gaming and reward scratch-written tasks. The change reshuffled the leaderboard and pushed more attention onto harness quality instead of raw model scores alone.
> Agent evals are getting closer to system evals. That matters for enterprise teams, because the wrapper, tool loop, sandbox, and fallback behavior often decide whether a strong model actually ships well.

### [[AINews] Kimi-K2.7-Code open-source release](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Moonshot released Kimi-K2.7-Code as an open coding-focused MoE model with reported gains over K2.6 and about 30% fewer reasoning tokens. The release highlights a 1T-parameter architecture with 32B active parameters and 256K context, with fast support from the inference ecosystem.
> This is the open-model story worth tracking today. Lower token burn plus fast toolchain support is how an open release moves from benchmark post to something teams can actually trial.
</div>

### [[AINews] MiniMax M3 arrives with 1M-token context](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
MiniMax released M3 as an open-weight multimodal MoE model with roughly 428B total parameters, around 23B active parameters, and a 1M-token context window. SGLang, vLLM, Together, Baseten, Fireworks, and GGUF tooling all moved quickly to support it.
> Long context is only interesting when deployment support lands fast enough for engineers to test real workloads. This one looks more operationally serious than a lot of open-weight launches.
</div>

### [[AINews] AA-AgentPerf benchmarks agentic inference](https://www.latent.space/p/ainews-fable-and-mythos-officially)
Artificial Analysis launched AA-AgentPerf, a benchmark for long-horizon coding agents that emphasizes production concerns like KV cache reuse, speculative decoding, and prefill/decode disaggregation. Its headline metric is Agents per Megawatt.
> This is where infra discussion gets real. Throughput alone is too shallow for agent systems, and power-normalized agent performance is closer to the metric platform teams will actually care about.
