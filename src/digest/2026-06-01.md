### [How we contain Claude across products](https://www.anthropic.com/engineering/how-we-contain-claude)
Anthropic outlined how it contains Claude across `claude.ai`, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork using sandboxes, VMs, egress controls, and scoped approvals. The post also shared failure cases, including pre-consent hook execution in Claude Code and the approval-fatigue problem that led to auto mode and stronger OS-level sandboxing.
> This is the kind of piece enterprise teams should read closely. The big signal is simple: model quality helps, but blast-radius design still does the heavy lifting when you let agents touch real systems.

### [GitHub Copilot’s new token-based billing spurs consternation among devs](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/what-a-joke-github-copilots-new-token-based-billing-spurs-consternation-among-devs/)
TechCrunch reports that GitHub Copilot is moving from flat-rate pricing to token-based billing on June 1, 2026, and some developers are reporting steep projected cost increases. The backlash centers on workloads that chain long premium requests and sub-agents, which can multiply usage quickly.
> Pricing is turning into architecture. If agent workflows stay opaque, finance and engineering will keep colliding over surprise bills. Teams need token budgets, observability, and workload tiering before these tools spread deeper into delivery pipelines.

### [SoftBank says it will invest up to €75 billion to build French data centers](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/softbank-says-it-will-invest-up-to-e75-billion-to-build-french-data-centers/)
SoftBank said it plans to invest up to €75 billion to build and operate as much as 5 gigawatts of new data center capacity in France, with 3.1 gigawatts targeted in the first phase by 2031. TechCrunch notes this is SoftBank’s largest AI infrastructure investment in Europe so far.
> The infrastructure race is still accelerating. Every enterprise AI roadmap now sits on top of power, land, cooling, and regional policy, whether teams like talking about that layer or not.
</div>

### [Cognition’s Scott Wu says AI coding agents shouldn’t replace humans](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/29/cognitions-scott-wu-says-ai-coding-agents-shouldnt-replace-humans/)
After Cognition raised $1 billion at a $26 billion valuation, CEO Scott Wu told TechCrunch that Devin is meant to help programmers build more, not replace them. He said the tool currently works somewhere between a junior and mid-level engineer depending on the task, while handling a large share of toil and maintenance work.
> That framing is smarter than the usual layoffs-first story. The useful question for most teams is not “can this replace engineers,” it’s “which parts of the software lifecycle can safely move to agent-assisted execution with human judgment still intact.”
</div>

### [Google’s 24/7 AI assistant Gemini Spark is actually pretty useful](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/30/i-put-googles-24-7-ai-assistant-gemini-spark-to-work-and-its-actually-pretty-useful/)
TechCrunch tested Gemini Spark, Google’s cloud-hosted always-on assistant for Gmail, Calendar, Docs, Sheets, and related tasks, and found it genuinely useful for research, reminders, and recurring workflows despite rough edges like broken links and missing Google Keep support. The review suggests Spark works best as a managed task runner across Google’s productivity stack.
> This looks like another step toward agent products that feel more like background services than chatbots. The real adoption test will be trust, handoff quality, and whether the workflow stays coherent when the task crosses app boundaries.
</div>
