### [Tencent Hy3](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/6/hy3/#atom-everything)
Tencent released Hy3 under Apache 2.0, a 295B-parameter MoE model with 21B active parameters, 256K context, and an FP8 quantized variant. Simon Willison highlighted Tencent's claim that it rivals much larger open models and noted free OpenRouter access through July 21.
> Open models keep getting stronger, and the interesting question is shifting from benchmark bragging to operational fit. If Hy3 is solid on tool use and long-context workloads, enterprise teams get another credible way to keep pricing and architecture options open.

### [Vercel CEO Guillermo Rauch on the fight to split off models from agents](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/06/vercel-ceo-guillermo-rauch-on-the-fight-to-split-off-models-from-agents/)
Guillermo Rauch told TechCrunch that Vercel now sees 6 million deployments a day, half driven by coding agents, with more than 1 trillion tokens flowing through its AI gateway daily. He argued enterprises are moving away from single-lab commitments and toward modular stacks with separate models, gateways, sandboxes, and data layers.
> The model is becoming one component in a larger control plane. That shift favors teams that treat observability, policy, and routing as first-class architecture, not plumbing.
</div>

### [The 'first' AI-run ransomware attack still needed a human](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/06/the-first-ai-run-ransomware-attack-still-needed-a-human/)
TechCrunch reported that Sysdig's JadePuffer case did automate the intrusion flow, but a human still picked the victim, provisioned infrastructure, and supplied stolen credentials. The agent exploited known flaws, moved through the environment, encrypted data, and wrote its own ransom note.
> Security teams should read this as a throughput story. Full attacker autonomy is still incomplete, and the practical cost of launching serious operations is still dropping.
</div>

### [Microsoft lays off nearly 5,000 employees across Xbox, commercial sales](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/06/microsoft-lays-off-nearly-5000-employees-across-xbox-commercial-sales/)
Microsoft cut about 4,800 roles, including roughly 1,600 at Xbox, while saying the eliminated jobs are not direct one-for-one AI replacements. TechCrunch tied the cuts to broader restructuring and Microsoft's continued push into enterprise AI deployments.
> The pattern is getting harder to ignore. AI investment is moving alongside leaner org structures, which means architecture choices now affect team design and operating cost, not just product capability.

### [The first American autonomous ground vehicles are fighting in Ukraine](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/07/the-first-american-autonomous-ground-vehicles-are-fighting-in-ukraine/)
Forterra said more than 100 self-driving ATVs have been deployed in Ukraine over the past nine months, logging more than 1,100 missions, over 2,500 miles, and 52 casualty evacuations. Soldiers still teleoperate them heavily in combat zones, while the company works on combining classical robotics with newer generative AI methods.
> Physical AI still gets judged by the hard stuff: data quality, communications resilience, and fallback control. The software story matters, and the system around the model matters more.
</div>
